# 14.6 Making Your Site AI-Friendly

I'll be the first to admit that this is a bit of a vague topic, but I wanted to include it in this course because I think it's important to start thinking about how to make your site friendly for AI tools, such as ChatGPT and other large language models (LLMs). These tools are becoming increasingly popular and are being used by many people to find information, generate content, and even interact with websites. If your site is not friendly for these tools, you may be missing out on a lot of potential traffic and engagement. On the other hand, depending on your goals for your site, you may not want to make it AI-friendly. You may instead want to block AI tools from accessing your site, which is also possible. In this tutorial, we'll briefly discuss some of the ways that you can make your site more friendly for AI tools, as well as some of the ways that you can block AI tools from accessing your site if that's what you want to do.

We've already seen that _Yoast SEO_ helps us with building an _llms.txt_ file, which is a file that provides information to LLMs about how to interact with your site. This is a great start, but there are other things that you can do to make your site more friendly for AI tools. For example, you can use structured data (such as Schema.org markup) to provide more information about your content in a way that is easy for AI tools to understand. You can also use clear and descriptive titles and headings, as well as alt text for images, to help AI tools understand the content of your site. Additionally, you can use a sitemap to help AI tools discover all of the pages on your site. All of these were also covered this week in our discussions of technical SEO, but it's worth mentioning again in the context of making your site AI-friendly.

One other thing that you might want to consider is provide Markdown versions of your content. Many AI tools are trained on large amounts of text data, and Markdown is a popular format for writing and formatting text. By providing Markdown versions of your content, you can make it easier for AI tools to understand and process your content. This can be especially helpful if your content includes a lot of formatting, such as lists, tables, or code snippets. There are various plugins available for WordPress that can help you generate Markdown versions of your content, such as [_Markdown Mirror_](https://wordpress.org/plugins/markdown-mirror/), [_LLM Markdown_](http://wordpress.org/plugins/llm-markdown/), and [_Serve Markdown_](https://wordpress.org/plugins/serve-md/). Each of these plugins has its own strengths and weaknesses, so you may want to try a few to see which one works best for your needs. Be sure to examine the approach they take to helping AI bots discover the Markdown versions of your content, as well as the format of the URLs for the Markdown versions, to ensure that they will work well with the AI tools that you want to target, your site structure, and the other plugins that you use. You may also need to tweak settings in different plugins after settling on one of these. For example, Yoast and Markdown Mirror both create llms.txt files, so you may need to adjust the settings in one or both of those plugins to ensure that the llms.txt file is correct and includes the appropriate information about the Markdown versions of your content.

Also, please note that there are additional plugins that help you import Markdown content into WordPress, but those are more focused on helping you write your content in Markdown and then convert it to HTML for display on your site, which is a different use case than providing Markdown versions of your content for AI tools to access.

What if you want to block AI tools from accessing your site? This is accomplished using meta tags or robots.txt rules that tell AI bots not to crawl or index your site - [with robots.txt being seemingly the best of the two options](https://raptive.com/blog/ai-bot-blocking-strategy/)(https://www.cloudflare.com/learning/ai/how-to-block-ai-crawlers/). Unfortunately, it's up to the bots to respect these rules. You can learn more about the process in [this article about robots.txt](https://www.iubenda.com/en/blog/block-openai-bard-crawlers/). There are various plugins available for WordPress that can help you do this, including [_Better Robots.txt_](https://wordpress.org/plugins/better-robots-txt/), and various SEO plugins provide this as an option, including the paid plan of _Yoast SEO_. Again, each of these plugins has its own strengths and weaknesses, so you may want to try a few to see which one works best for your needs. Another method for blocking AI bots is to use a Web Application Firewall (WAF) or a Content Delivery Network (CDN) that has features for blocking AI bots. [Cloudflare is one popular option that offers this kind of functionality.](https://www.cloudflare.com/learning/ai/how-to-block-ai-crawlers/).

This is a rapidly evolving area, and there are many different approaches to making your site AI-friendly or blocking AI bots from accessing your site. I encourage you to do some research and experimentation to find the approach that works best for your site and your goals.
