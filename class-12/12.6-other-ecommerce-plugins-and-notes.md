# 12.6 Other eCommerce Plugins and Notes

In this section, we’ll briefly look at WooCommerce and a few other eCommerce plugin options for WordPress.

## WooCommerce Overview

WooCommerce is a powerful and flexible plugin for building full-featured eCommerce sites. It supports:

* Multiple payment methods
* Complex workflows
* Extensive customization

If you need a more advanced online store, WooCommerce is likely a strong candidate.

You can explore its features at WooCommerce.com, and it’s worth watching some tutorials or demos to understand its full capabilities.

## Extensibility and Integrations

One of WooCommerce’s big strengths is its developer API. This allows you to:

* Build custom extensions
* Integrate with external systems
* Extend functionality beyond what’s available out of the box

There are also many pre-built extensions available. These cover areas such as:

* Payment providers
* Marketing tools
* Workflow automation
* Shipping and rate calculations

In many cases, integrations already exist for popular tools. If not, you can build your own.

## Other eCommerce Plugin Options

WooCommerce is just one option. If you search for “eCommerce” in WordPress, you’ll find many alternatives.

The right choice depends on your needs. For simpler use cases, a lightweight plugin might be a better fit.

## Final Thoughts

There are many eCommerce plugins available in WordPress, ranging from very simple to highly advanced.

* Use WooCommerce for flexibility and scalability
* Use simpler plugins for lightweight needs

Take some time to explore your options and choose the tool that best fits your project.
